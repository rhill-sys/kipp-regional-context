The NAEP adjustment: what would New York score on a PARCC-rigor test?

Two independent methods, same logic: find what New York's reported proficiency would look like if its bar were as high as New Jersey's. Method A — the NAEP twin. Among PARCC-legacy states, Maryland is New York's statistical twin on NAEP 2024 (within ~1–3 scale points in all four grade/subject cells; composite 244.1 vs 244.2) — and Maryland, on its PARCC-successor MCAP, reports 50.8% ELA and 26.5% math (2025). Illinois (NAEP slightly above NY) reported ~41% ELA / 28.4% math on its PARCC-derived IAR under its pre-2025 cuts — cuts ISBE then lowered precisely because they were among the nation's hardest. Method B — the NAEP-Proficient share. New Jersey's grade-4 math cut maps exactly at NAEP Proficient (249), and NJ's reported rate (45%) matches its NAEP-Proficient share (43.9%) almost perfectly — validating the method. New York's share of students at/above NAEP Proficient in G4 math is 36.6%, against a reported G4 state-test rate of 58%.
